    What you could do to improve your password strength:

    Nonsensical Phrases: Create a nonsensical phrase that doesn't relate to your personal information. For example: "PurpleElephant$Rainbow987".

    Take a phrase or sentence meaningful to you and turn it into an acronym while substituting some letters with numbers or symbols. For example: "I Love Dogs" could become "1L0v3@D0gg.5".

    Passphrase: Instead of a single word, use a combination of random words, numbers, and symbols that are easy for you to remember but difficult for others to guess. For example: "BlueMoon$42@Jump!"

    Length Matters: Generally, longer passwords are stronger. Aim for at least 12 characters, and ideally even more.
